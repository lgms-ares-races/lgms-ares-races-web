## Emergency Activities

If you are notified that a government agency has declared an Emergency Condition exists requiring our participation or you haven't been notified but believe that an applicable Emergency Condition exists, you should follow the procedures described in the following pages.

In the context of what follows, **Activation** is the trigger for your participation. **Deployment** is what you do once Activated.

## How long will I be needed?

Assignments will usually be for a minimum of 8 hours (one normal shift), or a maximum of 12 hours subject to personal needs, including those of your family. Volunteers should plan on working for one shift a day. Emergencies are expected to be up to 3 days (72 hours) until Public Safety personnel are able to restore normal coverage.

## What do I bring?

1. your go kit
2. your radio
3. food / drink
4. extra clothes

## Where do I go?

The attached file includes all locations within Los Gatos/Monte Sereno for which Amateur Radio equipment is planned to be used for Emergency Communications. Each LG/MS ARES-RACES member will deploy to one of the locations listed. Each person will have a default deployment location, but the best protocol to follow is to contact LG/MS A-R Resource Net (W6PIY: 147.390, + offset, Tx PL/CTCSS: 151.4Hz) for assignment. Assignments will be based on need an availability of personnel. In the event that you are unable to contact Resource Net, proceed to your default location.
